Members can withdraw from the ladder and go on "injured reserve" at any time for any reason. They can return anytime during the year with a 5-rung penalty. At the beginning of next year's season, "injured reserve" players are returned to the ladder with a 5-rung penalty, for the sake of bookkeeping.

NOTES

  • WINTER CUTBACK: The Har-Tru courts will be closed in stages through Thanksgiving. The lighted hard courts will be open all year.
  • TENNIS CENTER AT COLLEGE PARK: The TCCP is offering NASA employees and GTC members an opportunity to join at corporate rates ($50/month instead of $65/month) and play indoors ($20/hr). See http://rsd.gsfc.nasa.gov/tennisclub/tccp.html for details.
  • AUTUMN LEAVES: The bottom of the nets are being tied up for the fall to allow the leaves to blow under them, out to the fences. This make the leaves easier to remove from the courts.
  • Late in 2001, GEWA was considering building a racketball/handball facility by the tennis area.
  • USE'M OR LOSE'M: The ladder season ended October 31. Any unrestricted challenges that were not used by then are lost next season.
  • In September 2001, GSFC security began requiring employee picture-badges or personal escorts for admittance to the campus.
  • In September 2001, the International Tennis Federation voted to allow three different types of tennis ball according to the pace of the surface -- ball type 1 (fast speed), ball type 2 (medium speed) and ball type 3 (slow speed).
  • A new "super tiebreak" was also approved by thr ITF as an experiment until the end of next year to replace the deciding final set in a three or five set match. The first player to 10 points will win the new super tiebreak provided he leads by a margin of two points.
